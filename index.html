2. The Character

2.1 Attributes

Strength

Used whenever a character must use feats of strength or power. For example, moving large objects, kicking open a locked door, pulling up a load attached by rope from a well, climbing a rocky cliffside, etc.

Constitution

Represents the character’s resistance towards physical damage. For example, you get bludgeoned by a warhammer, then, based on your constitution, the damage you sustain will be lowered (or increased if your constitution is low). Constitution also determines character’s maximum health.

Willpower

Represents the character’s resistance towards magical damage. For example, a powerful wizard attempts to steal your sanity, however you have a high willpower and are able to resist his attack. Willpower also determines the character’s maximum spirit.

Intelligence

Used when the character is attempted to absorb knowledge or be intuitive. For example, the character is locked in a room with a single door as the exit. If he is aware that the door is trapped (see Awareness), then his intelligence would allow him to disarm the trap.

Speed

Used when the character must be fast or agile. For example, if the character opens a large stone door only to trigger its trap -- the floor collapses to an underground cavern -- his speed would allow him to try and quickly move to another part of the floor before he falls in.

Charisma

Used to determine the likability of the character. For example, if a character is trying to barter for a sword at a local weapons shop, his charisma might allow him to get it for cheaper if he seems friendly and polite to the shopkeep.

Awareness

Used to see if the character notices certain things. For example, if the character is searching a room for a special item, his awareness would tip him off to the location of the item.
